1) Who we are

VPN World is an independent information site about VPNs, privacy, and practical cybersecurity. This Privacy Policy explains what data may be processed when you use the site and how you can control it.

Privacy contact: u1797008805@gmail.com.

2) What data we process

Depending on your settings (consent) and how you use the site, VPN World may process:

  • Technical data (device type, browser, approximate region/country, referrer/source) — typically via analytics, only with consent.
  • Usage data (pages viewed, time on page, basic interaction events) — via GA4, only with consent.
  • Contact data you provide by email (your email address and the content of your message) if you get in touch.

VPN World does not require accounts, does not intentionally collect sensitive categories of data, and is designed to minimise data collection by default.

3) Cookies and consent controls

Cookies are small text files stored on your device. On VPN World, cookies / local storage are used mainly to:

  • Remember your consent choice (whether analytics is enabled) — stored locally as vpnworld_consent.
  • Run analytics (Google Analytics 4) — only after you consent.

Current consent status

Analytics is controlled via Google Consent Mode v2. Status: Denied

Withdrawing consent sets analytics_storage to denied and prevents analytics cookies being stored going forward. Existing cookies may remain until they expire or you delete them in your browser settings.

4) Analytics (Google Analytics 4)

If you consent, VPN World uses Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand which content is useful, where users struggle, and how to improve site structure (navigation, readability, section order).

  • Provider: Google (Google Analytics / Google Tag)
  • Purpose: traffic measurement and site improvement
  • Scope: page events, device info, traffic sources (in aggregated/statistical form)
  • Control: you can grant or withdraw consent at any time using the buttons above

5) Embedded content (YouTube etc.)

Where possible, we use privacy-enhanced embedding (for example youtube-nocookie.com) to reduce tracking until you interact with the player. After interaction, the third-party provider may process data according to its own policies.
