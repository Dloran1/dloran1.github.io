Quick verdict by use-case

  • Pure speed / gaming / large downloads: NordVPN (NordLynx is extremely consistent on UK endpoints).
  • Households with many devices: Surfshark (unlimited connections + good Smart DNS).
  • Netflix UK & BBC iPlayer reliability: slight edge to NordVPN for fewer proxy errors overall.
  • Lowest monthly cost: Surfshark generally cheaper on long plans.
  • Privacy fundamentals: both are audited no-logs; Nord adds Meshnet, Surfshark adds Rotating IP/Alternative ID.

Speed & protocols

On modern UK lines (FTTP 300–900 Mbps), both deliver excellent throughput over WireGuard-family protocols:

  • NordVPN: NordLynx (WireGuard variant) usually tops sustained speeds and latency stability.
  • Surfshark: WireGuard very close behind; strong multi-hop and good congestion handling.

For restrictive networks (hotel/office), both support OpenVPN TCP/443. See protocols guide.

Streaming (Netflix UK, BBC iPlayer, Sky Go)

  • NordVPN: fewer proxy/unblocker errors on UK evenings; quick rotation fixes. Guides: Netflix UK, iPlayer, Sky Go.
  • Surfshark: reliable as well; wins on Smart DNS for devices without VPN apps (consoles/TVs).

After switching locations, always fully quit and relaunch the streaming app to clear stale routes.

Privacy, audits & security

  • Both claim no-logs and have had independent audits. Prefer providers with repeat audits and RAM-only servers. Details: No-logs VPN.
  • Kill switch: present in both; enable system-wide mode if available.
  • Private DNS: keep “use provider DNS” on to prevent DNS leaks.
  • Extras: Nord’s Meshnet (secure devices-to-device), Surfshark’s Rotating IP and Alternative ID for account hygiene.

FAQ

Which is faster in the UK?

NordVPN usually edges out with NordLynx, especially for latency-sensitive tasks. Surfshark is close on WireGuard.

Which is better for Netflix UK and BBC iPlayer?

Both work; NordVPN has a small reliability edge. Always rotate servers and relaunch apps after a switch.

Which should I pick for a large household?

Surfshark — unlimited devices + handy Smart DNS for TVs and consoles.

Do both support a kill switch and split tunneling?

Yes. Enable the kill switch and use split tunneling to exclude UK banking or LAN apps.
