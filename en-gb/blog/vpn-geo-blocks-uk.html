Have you ever tried to watch a film on Netflix only to see “This content isn’t available in your country”? That’s a geo-block — a restriction that decides what you can access based on your location. Below you’ll find a practical, no-nonsense guide to how VPNs work, why geo-blocks exist, and the safest way to bypass them without sacrificing privacy.

What are geo-blocks and how do they work?

Websites and streaming platforms identify your region by checking your device’s IP address. IP ranges are allocated per country, so services can easily show UK catalogues to UK visitors and block others. When a platform detects a foreign IP for content licensed only in Britain, it returns errors or a trimmed library. This is why travellers often lose access to home services abroad.

Why companies use geo-blocking

  • Licensing rights — movie and sports rights are sold per territory.
  • Market pricing — regional price experiments and promotions.
  • Compliance — laws and local censorship in some countries.

How a VPN helps you bypass restrictions

A Virtual Private Network routes your traffic through an encrypted tunnel and swaps your visible IP address for one in another country. To streaming sites you look like a local viewer there, which unlocks libraries. For setup across the whole home, see the VPN router setup guide; for individual devices, start with Android, Windows or Apple TV.
