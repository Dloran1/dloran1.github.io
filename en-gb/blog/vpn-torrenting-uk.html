Quick setup (3 minutes)

  1. Install a trusted app: NordVPN / Surfshark (both support P2P).
  2. Protocol: WireGuard (NordLynx on NordVPN). Enable Kill switch, Auto-connect, and DNS leak protection.
  3. Connect to a nearby P2P-labelled UK (or NL) server. Start your client and verify your IP shows the VPN ASN, not your ISP.

Amsterdam/London routes are often fastest for UK users. If UK nodes are busy, try NL/FR and compare throughput/ping.

Why use a VPN for torrenting

  • 🛡️ Privacy: hides your real IP from swarms/trackers.
  • 🔒 Encryption: your ISP sees a VPN tunnel, not P2P flows.
  • 🚦 Anti-throttling: harder for traffic shapers to classify.
  • 📶 Stability: better reliability on shared/captive networks.

A VPN increases privacy and resilience — it isn’t a legal shield. Stick to lawful content. Streaming tips: BBC iPlayer (UK) • Netflix UK.

Client & app settings that matter

VPN app

  • Protocol: WireGuard/NordLynx for speed; OpenVPN UDP as fallback; OpenVPN TCP/443 only on strict networks.
  • Kill switch: ON (prefer system-wide). Test by killing the app mid-transfer — traffic should stop.
  • DNS/IPv6: use provider DNS; enable IPv6 leak protection.
  • Auto-connect: on Wi-Fi + mobile; mark trusted home SSID if you seed long-term.
  • Obfuscation: enable only if your network blocks VPNs (costs some speed).

qBittorrent (recommended)

  • Connections: global 400–600; per torrent 80–150 (lower for weak routers).
  • Transport: allow uTP + TCP (uTP helps with bufferbloat).
  • Encryption: Prefer; switch to Require only on hostile ISPs.
  • Port: random on start (50k–65k). If your VPN supports port forwarding, set that port here.
  • Bind to interface: bind to the VPN adapter to pause traffic if the tunnel drops.

uTorrent/BitTorrent

  • Randomise listening port; avoid defaults.
  • UPnP/NAT-PMP: ON if your VPN supports it; OFF for deterministic behaviour.
  • DHT/PEX/LSD: ON for public swarms; OFF on private trackers.

Beating UK ISP throttling

  • Rotate UK servers or try NL/FR (nearby, low latency).
  • Toggle WireGuard ↔ OpenVPN-UDP; some networks shape specific ports.
  • Change the client’s listening port to a high random range (50k–65k).
  • Fix Wi-Fi: 5 GHz or Ethernet; avoid congested channels.
  • Reduce concurrent torrents/connections on budget routers.

UK law: what’s allowed

BitTorrent is legal; sharing copyrighted material without permission is not. A VPN improves privacy and helps avoid throttling or profiling, but it doesn’t grant rights to redistribute protected content. Stick to Linux ISOs, open-source media, permitted mods, or your own files.
