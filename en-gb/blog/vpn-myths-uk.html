Quick answer: A VPN is a privacy tool, not a cure-all. Use a reputable service, enable a Kill Switch, configure it properly, and choose a nearby low-load server for the best speeds.

Myth 1: “A VPN always slows the internet down”

Speed loss mainly depends on server distance and load. Choose nearby servers and fast protocols; test realistically via VPN speed test.

Myth 2: “VPNs are illegal”

In most countries, using a VPN is legal — what matters is what you do online. Read: Is VPN legal in the UK?

Myth 3: “Free VPNs are as good as paid ones”

Free services often limit speed/data and may monetize usage data. See Free vs Paid VPN.

Myth 4: “A VPN makes me fully anonymous”

A VPN hides your IP, not browser fingerprints or cookies. Run checks with a DNS/IPv6 leak test.

Myth 5: “Any server works for streaming or P2P”

Streaming needs the right region and stable latency; P2P needs bandwidth and explicit policy support. Start with Which VPN server to choose.

Myth 6: “A VPN fixes Wi-Fi security issues”

It’s one layer only — secure your router (WPA3) and follow safe habits: see Wi-Fi security.

FAQ

Will a VPN always slow me down?

No — use a nearby low-load server and a fast protocol.

Is using a VPN legal?

Yes in most countries — always follow local laws and the site’s terms.

Are free VPNs as good as paid?

Usually not — expect limits and weaker privacy; consider a reputable paid option.
