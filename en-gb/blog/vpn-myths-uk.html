Myth 1: A VPN always slows you down dramatically

Not necessarily. Speed loss depends on distance and server load. With modern protocols like WireGuard and optimized routing, premium VPNs maintain near-native performance. See our VPN speed test guide.

Myth 2: Using a VPN is illegal

VPN use is legal in most countries. What matters is the activity, not the tool. Learn details in Is VPN legal in the UK?

Myth 3: Free VPNs are as good as paid ones

Free VPNs often limit bandwidth, collect data, or lack strong encryption. Compare them in our Free vs Paid VPN comparison.

Myth 4: VPN makes you 100% anonymous

VPN hides your IP, but browser fingerprints and cookies can still identify you. Always check for DNS and IPv6 leaks with our VPN leak test.

Myth 5: Any server is fine for streaming or P2P

Different servers have different policies and loads. For streaming, pick a close, stable location; for torrenting, use P2P-friendly servers. See how to choose the best VPN server.

Myth 6: VPN alone secures your Wi-Fi network

VPN protects traffic, but your Wi-Fi router matters too. Harden it with WPA3 and safe DNS settings from our Wi-Fi security guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a VPN always slow down your connection?

No. Modern protocols and smart server selection minimise speed loss. Proximity and network quality matter most.

Is using a VPN legal in the UK?

Yes — using a VPN is legal for privacy and security. Illegal actions remain illegal even through a VPN.

Are free VPNs safe to use?

Most free VPNs monetise data or lack encryption. Choose a trusted paid provider for real security.
