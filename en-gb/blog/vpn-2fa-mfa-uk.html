Best-practice setup (UK)

  1. Enable MFA on email first (Gmail/Outlook), then on banks, brokerage, and work SSO.
  2. Prefer Authenticator app + Security keys (two keys: primary & backup kept separately).
  3. Store printed recovery codes offline; add a secure contact method.
  4. On phones: protect the authenticator with biometrics and a strong device passcode.
  5. Consider a Dedicated IP if your company allows-lists IPs for admin panels/SSO.

VPN-specific tips (banking, SSO, email)

  • Banking: use a local UK server; if flagged, try the same city repeatedly to “build” reputation or temporarily disable VPN for verification.
  • SSO/Work: if your org checks IP, request allow-listing or use a Dedicated IP. Keep time on devices in sync (TOTP drift causes failures).
  • Email: enable security keys for phishing resistance; keep TOTP as backup.
  • Kill switch + provider DNS: prevent IP/DNS leaks during login flows. See VPN Security — UK.

Recovery & device loss

  • Print recovery codes and keep them offline (not in cloud notes).
  • Register two hardware keys (primary + backup).
  • Export TOTP seeds or use a secure, encrypted backup within your authenticator.
  • If you lose access during travel, contact support from a known device/IP if possible.

FAQ

What’s the best MFA combo for most UK users?

Authenticator app (TOTP) + two FIDO2 security keys. Keep printed recovery codes offline.

Does a VPN break 2FA?

No. Some services flag unusual IPs; use a UK server or a Dedicated IP for allow-lists. Keep device time in sync for TOTP.

Are SMS codes safe enough?

They’re better than nothing but vulnerable to SIM swap and delays abroad. Prefer authenticator apps or security keys.

Can I use MFA on Apple TV or consoles?

MFA applies to accounts (Netflix, Microsoft, PlayStation). Use Smart DNS or a router VPN for the device network side.
