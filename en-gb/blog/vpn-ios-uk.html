Quick setup (3 minutes)

  1. Install the provider’s iOS app from the App Store and sign in.
  2. Set Protocol: WireGuard/NordLynx (fastest in most UK cases).
  3. Enable Auto-connect on Untrusted Wi-Fi and optionally on cellular.
  4. Turn on IPv6/DNS leak protection (if app exposes the option).
  5. Connect to a nearby UK server (London/Manchester/Birmingham).

On strict office/hotel Wi-Fi, temporarily switch to OpenVPN TCP/443. See protocols guide.

Best iOS settings (UK)

✅

Auto-connect: On for untrusted Wi-Fi; consider “Always On” if your provider supports it.

✅

Kill switch: iOS enforces per-tunnel blocking via On-Demand rules in many apps — keep it enabled.

✅

Bypass list / Split tunneling: If a banking app dislikes VPN, use the provider’s Bypass (where available) or disconnect temporarily.

✅

Private Relay: Disable iCloud Private Relay while testing VPN reliability — it can interfere with DNS routing.

✅

Background App Refresh: Keep enabled for the VPN app to improve fast reconnects.

Protocols on iOS: WireGuard vs IKEv2 vs OpenVPN

  • WireGuard/NordLynx — fastest, quick reconnects after sleep/roaming; first choice for most users.
  • IKEv2 — extremely stable handoffs between Wi-Fi and 5G; great for commuting; fewer knobs for strict firewalls.
  • OpenVPN TCP/443 — best fallback on restrictive UK networks (looks like HTTPS), but a bit slower.

Netflix UK & BBC iPlayer on iPhone/iPad

  1. Select UK servers recommended for streaming in the app (if labelled).
  2. If you get a proxy message, rotate UK server (e.g., London ↔ Manchester) and relaunch the app.
  3. On hotel/office Wi-Fi, switch to OpenVPN TCP/443 and try again.

DNS/WebRTC leaks on iOS

iOS handles WebRTC differently from desktop browsers, but DNS misroutes can still expose your ISP. Ensure the VPN app owns DNS while connected and re-test after switching protocols.

  • Toggle IPv6 handling if your app offers it; mismatched stacks can cause leaks on some networks.
  • Force-quit and relaunch streaming apps after server changes — many cache endpoints.

Speed tips for UK networks

  • Protocol: Use WireGuard first; fallback to TCP/443 only for strict Wi-Fi.
  • Server distance: pick the closest UK region; avoid overloaded nodes.
  • Background traffic: pause iCloud/Photos syncs during streaming tests.
  • 5G vs Wi-Fi: if public Wi-Fi is congested, try 5G with WireGuard — often faster for short sessions.

FAQ

Which protocol is best on iOS?

WireGuard/NordLynx for speed and quick reconnects; IKEv2 is excellent for roaming; OpenVPN TCP/443 for strict Wi-Fi.

Does iOS have a real kill switch?

Provider apps use On-Demand rules to block traffic when the tunnel drops. Keep auto-connect enabled for best results.

Why does a UK streaming app still show errors?

Endpoint may be flagged or cache is stale. Rotate servers, relaunch the app, and try TCP/443 on restrictive networks.

Is using a VPN on iPhone legal in the UK?

Yes. VPNs are legal in the UK. Always follow platform terms and local laws.
