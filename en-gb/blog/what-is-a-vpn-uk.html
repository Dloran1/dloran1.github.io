The term VPN (Virtual Private Network) refers to a secure connection technology that shields your internet traffic from spying, censorship, and data collection. When using a VPN, all your data is encrypted and routed through a secure tunnel to a remote server – making it appear as if you’re browsing from that server’s country, not your own.

How Does a VPN Work?

Normally, your internet provider (ISP) can see every website you visit and every file you download. A VPN hides this activity by encrypting your data with strong algorithms like AES-256. Once connected, your traffic goes through the VPN server first, and only then to the target website. As a result, no one – not even your ISP – can see what you’re doing online.

VPN Protocols and Encryption

Modern VPNs use multiple secure protocols to balance performance and safety. Learn the specifics in our UK explainer on VPN protocols, but in short:

  • OpenVPN (TCP/UDP) – open-source and extremely secure, works on most platforms.
  • WireGuard – lightweight and very fast; many providers (e.g., NordVPN’s NordLynx) build on it.
  • IKEv2/IPSec – ideal for mobiles that switch Wi-Fi/4G/5G frequently.

Why People Use VPNs in 2025

  • Secure connections on public Wi-Fi in cafes, airports, and hotels;
  • Hide their IP address from trackers and advertisers;
  • Access region-blocked libraries (see VPN & geo-blocks);
  • Prevent data collection by ISPs and governments;
  • Work remotely and protect company data (see VPN for business).

VPN vs Proxy – Key Differences

Although both can change your apparent location, a VPN is far more secure. A proxy redirects traffic without encryption, while a VPN creates an encrypted tunnel that protects every application on your device, not just the browser. Dive deeper in our comparison: Proxy vs VPN.

Are VPNs Legal?

Yes — VPN use is 100% legal in the UK and EU, as long as you don’t use it for illegal activity. Streaming platforms might restrict VPN traffic in their T&Cs, but using a VPN itself is not a crime.

FAQ

Does a VPN slow my Internet?
Only slightly — modern protocols like WireGuard keep speed loss minimal.

Is a VPN safe for online banking?
Yes. It adds encryption against Wi-Fi snooping and man-in-the-middle attacks.

Can I use a VPN on a smart TV?
Yes — via a native app where available, or a router-level setup (guide).
