Quick setup (2 minutes)

  1. Install NordVPN or Surfshark from Google Play.
  2. Set protocol to WireGuard/NordLynx. Turn on Kill Switch.
  3. Open Android settings → Network & internet → VPN → tap the gear for your VPN → enable Always-on VPN and Block connections without VPN.
  4. Connect to a low-load UK server (London/Manchester). Test your IP/DNS.

New to VPNs? Start with What is a VPN? and our presets in Best VPN Settings (UK).

WireGuard vs OpenVPN on Android

  • WireGuard/NordLynx — fastest and most efficient for UK broadband/5G; quick to reconnect after sleep.
  • OpenVPN UDP — solid fallback when a network treats WireGuard oddly.
  • OpenVPN TCP/443 — “looks like HTTPS”; best for restrictive Wi-Fi at hotels/campuses/offices (expect a small speed trade-off).

Best app settings (UK)

1) Core toggles

  • Auto-connect: On for Wi-Fi and mobile. Trigger on boot if offered.
  • Kill Switch: On in-app. Also enforce system-wide as above (Always-on + Block without VPN).
  • Use provider DNS: On. Avoid ISP DNS to reduce proxy detections.
  • Obfuscation: Off by default; enable only if your network blocks VPNs.

2) Server selection

  • Prefer low-load UK servers near you. Rotate endpoints during peak hours.
  • For travel, select the target country and relaunch streaming apps after the switch.

3) App hygiene

  • Keep the VPN app exempt from battery optimisations: Settings → Apps → VPN app → Battery → Unrestricted.
  • Allow “VPN active” notification — Android is less likely to kill foreground services.

Split tunneling: what to exclude

Split tunneling gives you control. Typical UK setup:

  • Outside the VPN: banking/work apps that dislike VPN egress, local smart-home apps.
  • Inside the VPN: browsers, streaming, messaging.

Router user? Consider policy routing/dual SSIDs so only TVs/consoles go via VPN. See VPN Router Setup (UK) and VPN on a Router (overview).

Streaming (Netflix, BBC iPlayer, more)

  • Netflix UK: If you get a proxy message, rotate UK servers → force-quit Netflix → relaunch. On strict Wi-Fi try TCP/443 (if app supports) or via a router hotspot. Full playbook: Netflix UK with a VPN.
  • BBC iPlayer: Use recommended UK endpoints; relaunch the app after each server change. Guide: BBC iPlayer with a VPN.
  • Smart TV handoff: Cast/Chromecast from Android while the router runs the VPN, or install the VPN app on Android TV. See Smart TV (UK).

Battery & stability tips

  • Use WireGuard — it’s CPU-light and reconnects quickly.
  • Disable aggressive battery optimisation for your VPN app.
  • Prefer 5 GHz Wi-Fi or Ethernet (for TV boxes). Jittery Wi-Fi causes renegotiations.
  • Don’t force-quit the VPN — let it run as a foreground service.

IP/DNS/WebRTC leaks on Android

Test after the first install and after app/OS updates. If a site shows ISP DNS or your real IP:

  • Reconnect the VPN and ensure “Use provider DNS” is ON.
  • Toggle Airplane Mode ON/OFF to reset radios if routes are stale.
  • Switch server/city; relaunch the target app.
