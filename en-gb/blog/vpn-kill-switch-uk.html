What is a Kill Switch?

It’s a safeguard that **cuts your internet connection** if the VPN disconnects. Without it, even a brief drop exposes your real IP and DNS requests to your ISP or websites.

There are two main approaches:

  • System-level: enforced at OS level, no traffic passes outside the VPN interface.
  • App-level: handled by the VPN app; can fail if the app crashes.

Why it matters (UK context)

  • Streaming: Without a Kill Switch, BBC iPlayer or Netflix may suddenly see your real UK IP mid-session and block you.
  • Torrents: Even a few leaked packets expose your identity to peers. Kill Switch prevents that.
  • Public Wi-Fi: If the VPN drops, your device reverts to the hotel/café network unencrypted. A Kill Switch ensures zero leakage.
  • Work from home: Sensitive corporate apps (SSO, RDP) may leak if VPN drops. Kill Switch keeps them locked down.

On Windows/macOS, look for **system-wide Kill Switch** in settings. On iOS/Android, enable “Block internet without VPN”.

How to enable on Windows, macOS, iOS, Android

Windows 11/10

  • In NordVPN/Surfshark app → Settings → Security → Kill Switch: On.
  • Prefer “system-wide” mode (blocks all traffic, not just app traffic).
  • Optional: enforce via Windows Firewall (advanced users).

macOS

  • App → Preferences → Kill Switch = On.
  • Enable **Firewall** in System Settings → Privacy & Security.
  • Test by disabling Wi-Fi mid-session.

iOS

  • Settings → VPN profile → enable **Connect on Demand** + Block without VPN.
  • Or in app: Kill Switch toggle.

Android

  • Settings → Network → VPN → gear icon → Always-on VPN + Block connections without VPN.
  • Test by toggling Airplane mode quickly.

Router-level Kill Switch

On OpenWRT/DD-WRT/pfSense: configure firewall rules so if VPN interface goes down, WAN traffic is dropped. Many providers supply ready rules.

Policy routing: send only consoles/TVs via VPN, others via WAN; but apply kill-rules to VPN-bound devices.

How to test if it works

  1. Connect VPN → start a download or video stream.
  2. Force-quit VPN app or disable interface.
  3. Observe: with Kill Switch ON, all traffic should stop. Without, traffic continues via ISP.
  4. Confirm at ipleak.net or browserleaks.com.

FAQ

What’s the difference between Kill Switch and Always-on VPN?

Always-on VPN reconnects automatically. Kill Switch ensures nothing leaks in the gap.

Does Kill Switch affect gaming latency?

No. It only activates when the tunnel drops. During normal play it has no overhead.

Is Kill Switch legal in the UK?

Yes. It’s just a security feature — fully legal.

Should I use Kill Switch on mobile data?

Yes, especially on public Wi-Fi or LTE/5G when privacy matters. But note the extra battery use.
