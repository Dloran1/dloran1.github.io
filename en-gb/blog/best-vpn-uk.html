How to choose a UK VPN

  1. Speed first: prioritise WireGuard/NordLynx; test evening performance.
  2. Streaming reliability: Netflix UK & iPlayer should work after server rotation and an app restart. See Netflix guide and iPlayer guide.
  3. Privacy: audited no-logs, reliable kill switch, strong DNS/WebRTC leak protection (leak checks).
  4. Devices: Windows/macOS/iOS/Android/Smart TV; consider unlimited devices if you share.
  5. Support: 24/7 chat that actually helps with UK servers & streaming issues.

Streaming in the UK (Netflix, BBC iPlayer)

Both Netflix and iPlayer change filters over time. A good UK VPN won’t promise magic — it provides workable methods that you can repeat:

  • Rotate to a different UK city/server when you see the proxy/unblocker message.
  • Switch protocol to OpenVPN TCP/443 on restrictive networks (details in protocols guide).
  • Relaunch the app after each change; clear app data on Smart TVs if needed.

See specifics: Netflix UK with a VPN and BBC iPlayer with a VPN.

Speed & protocols

WireGuard/NordLynx is the fastest in most UK broadband/5G cases. For stubborn networks, OpenVPN TCP/443 often looks like normal HTTPS and helps. On iOS while roaming, IKEv2 can be very stable.

Performance tuning: follow our Best VPN Settings (UK).

Smart TV & router tips

  • Use Ethernet for TVs/consoles where possible — less jitter, better 4K stability.
  • If an app sticks to an old region, clear its data or reinstall, then reboot the device.
  • Router VPN covers devices without native apps; see VPN Router Setup (UK) and VPN on a Router.

Privacy & no-logs (what matters)

  • Independent audits of no-logs claims
  • Kill switch that actually kills traffic on drops
  • DNS/WebRTC leak protection (test via our leak guide)
  • Jurisdiction & transparency (warrant canaries, security reports)
