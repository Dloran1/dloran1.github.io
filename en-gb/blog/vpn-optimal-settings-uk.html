A VPN can do much more than simply hide your IP. With the right configuration, you can unlock faster speeds, prevent DNS or IPv6 leaks and keep your browsing private even on public Wi-Fi. This 2025 UK guide explains each key setting in detail so you can configure your VPN once — and forget about it.

Get NordVPN — Fast & Private

1 — Choose the right protocol

Modern VPNs support several tunnelling protocols. For most UK users, WireGuard or NordLynx offers the best mix of speed and security. OpenVPN UDP remains reliable but slightly slower. Avoid outdated options like PPTP or L2TP/IPsec.

2 — Enable the Kill Switch

This feature stops your device from sending unencrypted data if the VPN connection drops. In the UK, ISPs log connections by default; a kill switch prevents accidental exposure.

3 — Use Auto-Connect on start-up

Let your VPN connect automatically when your device boots or when joining an unknown Wi-Fi. It ensures protection even on public hotspots at cafés or trains.

4 — Pick the nearest or least-loaded server

Distance affects latency. Use your provider’s speed test or “Quick Connect” to pick the lowest-ping server. If you stream or torrent, specialised P2P or streaming servers may perform better.

5 — Activate DNS & IPv6 Leak Protection

Always enable DNS and IPv6 leak protection in settings. Without them, requests could bypass the VPN tunnel and reveal your location. Check your setup using our DNS/IPv6 test guide.

6 — Turn on Threat Protection or blocker features

Advanced VPNs include malware filters and ad-blockers. They help stop phishing domains before they load, improving both speed and safety.

7 — Use Split Tunnelling carefully

This lets you exclude selected apps (like online games or banking) from the VPN. It improves latency but can expose real IPs — use only when necessary.

8 — When to change settings

  • Switch protocol if streaming feels slow
  • Update app versions — old builds often mis-handle DNS queries
  • Reset configuration after major OS updates

FAQ — Optimal VPN Settings (UK 2025)

Will these settings slow my internet?

No. Using WireGuard-based protocols with nearby servers keeps speeds close to normal broadband rates (usually within 5-10 %).

Which VPN protocol is best in 2025?

WireGuard or NordLynx for most UK users. OpenVPN UDP is a solid fallback; avoid PPTP and SSTP due to outdated encryption.

How do I prevent DNS leaks?

Enable “DNS Leak Protection” and use your provider’s secure DNS servers. Test occasionally with our check tool.

Do I need special settings on mobile?

Yes — set auto-connect on Wi-Fi and mobile data, use IKEv2 or WireGuard for stability, and enable “VPN on demand”.

What are the recommended default settings?

Protocol: WireGuard / NordLynx • Kill Switch: On • Auto-Connect: On • DNS Leak Protection: On • Threat Protection: On • Split Tunnelling: Optional.
