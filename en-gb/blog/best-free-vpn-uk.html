1. Free VPNs that are actually trustworthy in 2025

Look for services with open-source clients, clear privacy policies and verified audits. Reputable names usually offer a limited tier with no ads. Learn about transparency standards in our How VPN works guide.

2. What “free” really means

Most free VPNs limit bandwidth (2–10 GB monthly) or cut off after a few hours daily. Some monetise by injecting ads or selling analytics data. Our Free vs Paid VPN comparison explains why that matters.

3. Security checklist for free VPN users

  • Confirm AES-256 encryption and no DNS leaks (test here).
  • Prefer WireGuard or OpenVPN protocols over unknown ones.
  • Avoid apps asking for location or contact permissions.

4. Free VPNs for streaming (rare but possible)

Some providers whitelist UK media domains for BBC iPlayer or ITVX. Speed may fluctuate, but it works occasionally. When buffering annoys you, a paid plan with 10 Gbps servers solves it instantly.

5. Free VPN for public Wi-Fi

Perfect use-case for a free tier: encrypting traffic in cafés, airports or hotels. Combine it with a browser that clears cookies and you’ll avoid basic tracking. See also our Wi-Fi security guide.

6. Privacy trade-offs you should know

“No-logs” claims mean little without audits. Even good free VPNs may store session metadata to enforce limits. That’s why audited providers like NordVPN or Surfshark are safer choices long term.

7. When to switch to a paid VPN

Upgrade when you need multi-device support, streaming reliability, or unlimited bandwidth. Paid plans add kill switches, 24/7 support and fast protocols (WireGuard / NordLynx). Details in How VPN works.

FAQ

Is there a completely free VPN for the UK?

A few providers offer secure free tiers (ProtonVPN, Windscribe), but with limited speed and servers.

Can a free VPN be safe for banking?

Use only well-audited ones with HTTPS enabled. Otherwise choose a paid service for full encryption and support.

Will a free VPN work on my smart TV?

Rarely. Most free apps don’t support routers or TVs. A router-level setup with a paid VPN is better.
