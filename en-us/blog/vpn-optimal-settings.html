Quick summary: The best VPN setup for most US users is: WireGuard or NordLynx protocol, Auto server, Kill Switch ON, DNS/IPv6 leak protection ON, Auto-connect ON, and Split tunneling OFF. Tweak only if you need streaming, torrenting, or advanced security modes.

1. Pick the right protocol

WireGuard (or vendor version like NordLynx) is the fastest modern option. If it’s blocked or unstable, switch to OpenVPN UDP for balance or TCP for reliability. Avoid outdated PPTP/L2TP/IPsec.

  • WireGuard — best mix of security and speed.
  • OpenVPN UDP — reliable fallback.
  • OpenVPN TCP — for restrictive or corporate networks.

2. Server selection: US & nearby regions

Start with automatic selection, then manually test low-latency servers. East/West coast load often differs; switch coasts if needed. For international content, connect to a country that matches the service region.

3. Use the Kill Switch & Auto-connect

These prevent leaks if your connection drops or your PC wakes from sleep. Enable both options and allow the app to reconnect automatically on public Wi-Fi.

4. DNS & IPv6 leak protection

Always enable your provider’s DNS protection and disable IPv6 traffic if your VPN supports it. Test configuration using our VPN DNS leak test.

5. Split tunneling (advanced users)

Keep split tunneling off unless you need certain apps (like local banking or games) to bypass the VPN. When used, ensure sensitive apps stay protected. See our VPN for remote work guide.

6. Streaming and speed optimization

Use streaming-optimized servers for Netflix, Hulu, Disney+ or BBC iPlayer. Refresh your IP if buffering occurs and test speed differences as shown in VPN speed test.

7. Port forwarding & local network rules

Only enable port forwarding if required (e.g., torrent clients or NAS). If unsure, keep it off for safety. Read VPN port forwarding explained for details.

8. Public Wi-Fi profile

On hotel or airport Wi-Fi, always activate Kill Switch, Auto-connect, and Firewall ON. Disable printer and file sharing. See VPN for public Wi-Fi.

9. Troubleshooting common issues

  • Slow speed? Switch from TCP→UDP or try another nearby city.
  • Blocked sites? Enable obfuscation/stealth mode if your VPN supports it.
  • No internet after connect? Toggle DNS protection off→on or restart adapter.

FAQ — Optimal VPN Settings (US)

Will these settings slow my internet?

Not significantly. With WireGuard/NordLynx and a nearby server, overhead is typically under 10% on modern broadband.

What’s the best VPN protocol in 2025?

WireGuard/NordLynx for most users. Fall back to OpenVPN UDP for compatibility or TCP for reliability on restrictive networks.

How do I prevent DNS or IPv6 leaks?

Enable the app’s DNS leak protection and IPv6 blocking, then run a quick check using our DNS leak guide.

Do I need special settings on mobile?

Yes: turn on auto-connect on Wi-Fi and mobile data, prefer WireGuard/IKEv2 for stability, and keep the kill switch enabled.

What are the recommended defaults?

Protocol: WireGuard/NordLynx • Server: Auto (nearest) • Kill Switch: ON • DNS/IPv6 protection: ON • Auto-connect: ON • Split tunneling: OFF.
