Geo-blocking decides what you can watch or read online. It’s why some Netflix titles disappear abroad or why BBC iPlayer stops at the UK border. Your IP address reveals location — and websites act accordingly. But with a VPN you can encrypt traffic, change virtual location, and access the content you already pay for — without compromising privacy.

Why geo-blocking exists

  • Licensing rules: studios sell regional rights per country.
  • Price strategy: different markets, different pricing models.
  • Regulations: local censorship or compliance mandates.

How a VPN bypasses restrictions

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) reroutes your connection through an encrypted tunnel and swaps your IP with one from another region. Streaming services then see you as a local viewer there — unlocking shows, live sports, and websites worldwide. For full-home coverage, see the VPN Router Setup.

Beyond unblocking — privacy & security

VPNs don’t just open Netflix. They hide your IP, encrypt Wi-Fi connections, and prevent data profiling. Travelers use them to secure airport Wi-Fi or bank safely abroad. Learn more in Wi-Fi Security Guide and Free vs Paid VPN.

Free VPNs — what to know

Most free VPNs are quickly detected and blocked by streaming sites. Some collect user data or show ads. Paid services with audited no-logs policies (NordVPN, Surfshark) offer consistent speed and security worth the small fee.
