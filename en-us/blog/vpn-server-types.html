1. Standard VPN Servers

These are default VPN nodes that route your data through a single encrypted tunnel. They‚Äôre fast, reliable, and great for browsing securely on public Wi-Fi. To understand how they encrypt traffic, check vpn-protocols.html.

2. P2P Servers (for torrenting)

P2P servers are designed for high-bandwidth file sharing and uploading. They prevent IP exposure during torrenting. Top providers like NordVPN and Surfshark offer them in countries like the Netherlands and Switzerland where P2P traffic is protected.

3. Streaming Servers

These servers maintain access to major streaming platforms such as Netflix, Hulu, BBC iPlayer, and Disney+. They use rotating IPs to avoid being blacklisted by streaming platforms ‚Äî unlike regular servers that often trigger proxy errors.

4. Double VPN Servers

Traffic is routed through two VPN servers in sequence, giving you double encryption. It slightly reduces speed but maximizes anonymity ‚Äî ideal for journalists or activists. See vpn-double-vpn.html.

5. Dedicated IP Servers

They provide you with a private IP address that no one else uses ‚Äî reducing CAPTCHAs and login flags. Banks and corporate systems recognize it as a trusted connection. More in vpn-dedicated-ip.html.

6. Obfuscated Servers

They disguise VPN traffic to look like normal HTTPS data, bypassing VPN bans and firewalls. Perfect for school networks or regions that restrict VPNs.

7. Onion Over VPN Servers

This advanced setup first encrypts your data through a VPN tunnel and then routes it over the Tor network, adding multiple layers of encryption. Best for whistleblowers and security researchers who prioritize privacy over speed.

8. Business and Remote Work Servers

Optimized for stable enterprise connections and remote teams. These servers balance security and uptime for accessing corporate resources. See vpn-remote-work.html.
